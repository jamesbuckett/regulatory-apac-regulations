Method & Framing

Twelve dimensions, four severity grades

  • Twelve dimensions per jurisdiction — regulator identity through residency, isolation, cryptography, binding legislation.
  • Citations name the circular or master direction that constrains architecture — not the headline statute.
  • Strict — hard localization or onshore-only processing mandated by statute or binding circular.
  • Moderate — approval, mirroring, or supervisory-access conditions; cross-border permitted.
  • Light — principles-based expectations; notification or best-effort onshore preference.
  • None — no explicit mandate; industry practice and commercial considerations apply.

The brief distinguishes binding rules (statute / circular) from supervisory expectation (letters, guidance, examination practice) and industry practice (de facto standards). Areas of active regulatory flux are marked Flux.

Each country section closes with an Architectural Implications callout — five design constraints a practitioner would apply today. The document ends with a comparative matrix across the four localization / isolation dimensions that most materially determine deployment topology.

At a Glance — Localization & Isolation Heat Map

Regional takeaways — 30-second read

  • Strictest tier — China is strict on all four dimensions; India is strict on data and process.
  • Lightest tier — Australia, Singapore and Japan are permissive on data and entity rules, but all three hold a moderate workload-isolation floor.
  • Hidden trap — Hong Kong and Thailand rate Light on data but Moderate on workload isolation; easy to under-scope segregation.
  • Most active flux (2024–2026) — India DPDP rules, Indonesia PDP phase-in, Australia CPS 230 in force, Hong Kong CFI 2.0 rollout.

India's nuance: strict on data and process but moderate on entity and workload isolation.

Jurisdiction 1 of 10 · Domains 01–06

Indonesia (1/3)

  • 01 Regulatory Bodies — OJK (prudential & conduct), BI (payments), Kominfo (data), BSSN (cyber).
  • 02 Data Localization — BI 19/14/PBI/2017: payment data onshore; POJK 11/2022 allows offshore with approval.
  • 03 System & Process Localization — GPN switching, clearing & settlement onshore; onshore DR preferred.
  • 04 Legal Entity Segregation — local PT subsidiary or KCBA branch; fintechs must be Indonesian entities.
  • 05 Workload Isolation — public cloud permitted; payment switching and keys dedicated, with onshore HSM.
  • 06 Cross-Border Data Flow — PDP Law 27/2022: adequacy, safeguards or consent; BI restricts payment data.

02 — GR 71/2019 relaxed the general mandate, splitting public-scope (government) from private-scope ESOs; financial services keep tighter sectoral rules. Bank customer / transaction data may be mirrored offshore with an onshore primary copy and regulator access.

03 — Core payment authorization and fraud screening on domestic rails execute onshore; regional DR permitted for non-payment workloads with OJK consent; cross-border admin / DevOps allowed under OJK-approved access controls.

04 — Intra-group outsourcing permitted under POJK 9/POJK.03/2016 with documented oversight and local board accountability; local director accountability for IT risk is supervisory practice.

05 — Logical tenancy isolation acceptable for customer PII when supported by controls; production / non-production segregation explicit in OJK TRM. 06 — insurance and capital-markets carve-outs largely align with banking TRM.

Jurisdiction 1 of 10 · Domains 07–12

Indonesia (2/3)

  • 07 Cybersecurity Standards — POJK 11/2022 (ISO 27001 / NIST-aligned); OJK incidents ~24 h, BI disruptions 1 h.
  • 08 API & Open Banking — SNAP standard mandatory for payment providers; OAuth 2.0, mTLS, signed payloads.
  • 09 Cloud & Infrastructure — material outsourcing needs OJK notification and exit plans; payment CSPs onshore.
  • 10 Licensing & Compliance Tech — AML per Law 8/2010 (PPATK); e-KYC via Dukcapil biometrics; IKD digital ID.
  • 11 Encryption & Authentication — TLS 1.2+; customer MFA mandatory; onshore HSM for payment-switching keys.
  • 12 Key Legislation — UU ITE, GR 71/2019, PDP Law 27/2022, POJK 11/2022, BI PBI payment rules.

07 — Annual penetration testing, quarterly vulnerability scans; red-teaming is a supervisory expectation for systemically important banks.

08 — SNAP (Standar Nasional Open API Pembayaran, BI 2021) has phased onboarding; data-sharing beyond payments stays voluntary / industry-led. 09 — concentration risk addressed via demonstrable alternative-provider plans.

10 — e-KYC under POJK 12/POJK.03/2018 (digital banking) with biometric liveness. 11 — BI payment rails prescribe cipher suites and key-rotation intervals; no mandated national algorithm, SNI / international standards accepted.

12 — UU ITE is Law 11/2008 as amended by Law 19/2016 and Law 1/2024; PBI 23/6/PBI/2021 covers payment service providers.

Jurisdiction 1 of 10 · Architectural Implications

Indonesia (3/3)

  • Payment workloads onshore — dedicated VPC, onshore HSM, primary and DR inside Indonesia.
  • Non-payment workloads may process cross-border with OJK notification — design a clean data-classification boundary.
  • SNAP-compliant API gateway with mTLS and signed JWS payloads; key material stays onshore.
  • GPN / QRIS routing gateway inside Indonesia — foreign switching not permitted for domestic transactions.
  • Incident pipelines — 24-hour OJK notification and 1-hour BI disruption channel — must survive a CSP outage.

The classification boundary exists so only non-sensitive, non-payment data (CRM, analytics, collaboration) exits the country. Both regulator-notification channels are architectural dependencies in their own right: they must keep working while the cloud provider is down.

Jurisdiction 2 of 10 · Domains 01–06

India (1/3)

  • 01 Regulatory Bodies — RBI (banks, payments), SEBI, IRDAI, CERT-In (cyber), NPCI (UPI rails).
  • 02 Data Localization — RBI circular (6 Apr 2018): payment-system data stored only in India.
  • 03 System & Process Localization — domestic authorization, clearing, settlement in India; NPCI on-soil hosting.
  • 04 Legal Entity Segregation — Indian-incorporated payment aggregators; local CIO / CISO and board IT committee.
  • 05 Workload Isolation — IT Governance Direction 2023: segregation; dedicated core-banking and HSM environments.
  • 06 Cross-Border Data Flow — DPDP 2023 negative-list model (rules pending); payment localization overrides.

02 — RBI Circular DPSS.CO.OD.No. 2785/06.08.005/2017-18 applies to all payment-system operators including foreign schemes; KYC documents keep an onshore primary with offshore mirroring permitted under access and deletion guarantees.

03 — NPCI requires direct connectivity into its Mumbai / Hyderabad / Chennai data centres; DR must be in India in a different seismic zone; overseas admin access must be governed, logged and CERT-In compliant.

04 — Foreign banks operate as branches or wholly-owned subsidiaries; PA-PG Master Direction (2020, updated 2024). Group platforms allowed under the outsourcing direction with effective local control.

05 — Zero-trust not explicit but strongly aligned with the Digital Payment Security Controls direction. 06 — SEBI Cloud Framework (2023) and IRDAI outsourcing rules add accessibility and audit rights.

Jurisdiction 2 of 10 · Domains 07–12

India (2/3)

  • 07 Cybersecurity Standards — CERT-In: 6-hour incident reporting, 180-day onshore logs; SEBI CSCRF (2024).
  • 08 API & Open Banking — Account Aggregator consent framework; UPI; OAuth 2.0, signed JWS, mTLS to NPCI.
  • 09 Cloud & Infrastructure — RBI draft cloud framework (2024); SEBI framework sets CSP selection and audit.
  • 10 Licensing & Compliance Tech — PMLA / FIU-IND reporting; Aadhaar e-KYC and video KYC; CKYCR registry.
  • 11 Encryption & Authentication — TLS 1.2+; 2FA for card-not-present; CCA-approved signatures; FIPS L3 HSMs.
  • 12 Key Legislation — IT Act 2000, DPDP Act 2023, RBI Payment Security & IT Governance Directions.

07 — RBI Cyber Security Framework in Banks (2016) plus Master Direction on Digital Payment Security Controls (2021); CERT-In Directions (28 Apr 2022) also mandate NTP sync to NPL/NIC; annual red-teaming for SCB/PSB, VAPT twice-yearly, ISO 27001 and PCI-DSS widespread.

08 — The AA model is an RBI-regulated NBFC-AA consent architecture (ReBIT specs); UPI is ISO 20022-aligned with 2.0 / AutoPay extensions; ONDC (Beckn protocol) influences payment orchestration; FAPI-like profiles being adopted.

09 — MeitY empanelment historically for government workloads; banks pick STQC / empanelled providers for sensitive workloads. 11 — keys held onshore for payment-system functions. 12 — includes SPDI Rules 2011 and CERT-In Directions 2022; DPDP rules notified Jan 2025.

Jurisdiction 2 of 10 · Architectural Implications

India (3/3)

  • All payment data — at rest, in processing, in backup — resides in India: CBS, switch and HSM with in-country DR.
  • Strict classification and tokenisation so overseas analytics see only non-payment, de-identified records.
  • CERT-In-aligned logging: 180-day onshore retention, NPL/NIC time sync, a 6-hour incident workflow.
  • Account Aggregator consent flows and RBI-compliant video-KYC; CKYCR as the canonical KYC store.
  • Treat cross-border movement as policy-controlled egress — expect a restricted subset under DPDP rules.

The DPDP negative list was still draft as of Jan 2025 — architect on the assumption that some categories of personal data will be barred from leaving India, so egress must be a controllable policy point rather than an ambient capability.

Jurisdiction 3 of 10 · Domains 01–06

Taiwan (1/3)

  • 01 Regulatory Bodies — FSC (integrated), CBC (central bank), NCC / MODA (ICT), PIPC (privacy, phasing in).
  • 02 Data Localization — no general statute; core banking data onshore by supervisory expectation.
  • 03 System & Process Localization — core platform and ledger onshore with onshore DR; FISC rails on-soil.
  • 04 Legal Entity Segregation — branch or subsidiary; subsidiaries required for securities and insurance.
  • 05 Workload Isolation — public cloud for non-core; core banking, AML, card issuance dedicated or isolated.
  • 06 Cross-Border Data Flow — PDPA Art. 21 lets FSC restrict transfers; cross-strait under national-security review.

02 — FSC can restrict specific datasets by sectoral notification under PDPA Art. 21; credit information (JCIC) and NCC Taiwan rails are strictly onshore.

03 — Domestic clearing runs through Financial Information Service Co. (FISC) and the TWD large-value RTGS (CBC); offshore processing of non-core functions needs FSC outsourcing pre-approval.

04 — Group-platform sharing allowed with pre-approved outsourcing; local board-level IT governance required. 05 — production / non-production segregation and privileged access audited in annual FSC examinations; the CSP market pairs domestic operators (Chunghwa Telecom) with hyperscale regions.

06 — otherwise individual consent plus purpose limitation.

Jurisdiction 3 of 10 · Domains 07–12

Taiwan (2/3)

  • 07 Cybersecurity Standards — FSC e-banking security regulations; Action Plan 4.0 red-teaming; ~2 h reporting.
  • 08 API & Open Banking — voluntary three-phase framework via FISC; FAPI-aligned, mTLS, OAuth 2.0.
  • 09 Cloud & Infrastructure — FSC pre-approval for material cloud; on-site audit rights; documented exit.
  • 10 Licensing & Compliance Tech — AML under Money Laundering Control Act; eKYC via TWID / JCIC; FIDO liveness.
  • 11 Encryption & Authentication — TLS 1.2+, MFA mandatory; GCA / TWCA national PKI; FIPS L3 HSMs onshore.
  • 12 Key Legislation — Banking Act Art. 45-1, PDPA, Cyber Security Management Act 2018, FSC outsourcing rules.

07 — The FSC Regulations Governing Standards for Information System & Security Management of Electronic Banking prescribe controls, logging and incident reporting; ISO 27001 and PCI-DSS widely adopted; ~2 hours applies to material service disruption.

08 — Phases: (1) public information, (2) customer information with consent, (3) transaction information. 09 — outsourcing regulations amended Jul 2019 with 2022 clarifications; SOC 2 / ISAE 3402 reports plus local attestations acceptable in lieu of on-site audit.

10 — MJIB acts as FIU; eKYC also runs through the National Credit Card Center. 11 — RSA-2048 / ECDSA-P256 for signatures; Electronic Signatures Act amended 2024. 12 — PDPA dates from 1995, amended 2023.

Jurisdiction 3 of 10 · Architectural Implications

Taiwan (3/3)

  • Core banking, ledger and customer master onshore with Taiwan DR; auditable FSC access for CSP-hosted components.
  • FSC pre-approval for material cloud outsourcing — deliver reversibility and exit design at approval, not after.
  • Integrate FISC rails and JCIC over onshore private connectivity; no cross-region routing of domestic settlements.
  • FAPI-grade open-banking façade, aligned to the FISC phase applicable to the institution's licence.
  • Design consent capture, purpose-limitation tagging and cross-border hooks now — PIPC is still maturing.

PIPC was established in 2024 with phased operationalization — the retrofit cost of consent and purpose-limitation plumbing is the reason to build the hooks before the regulator finishes standing up.

Jurisdiction 4 of 10 · Domains 01–06

Japan (1/3)

  • 01 Regulatory Bodies — FSA (regulator), BOJ (BOJ-NET), PPC (APPI), FISC (de facto security guidelines).
  • 02 Data Localization — no statutory mandate; FSA access on request; audit rights for offshore data.
  • 03 System & Process Localization — BOJ-NET and Zengin on-soil; core ledger and AML onshore by convention.
  • 04 Legal Entity Segregation — branches common; named local CIO / CISO accountability; FSA ring-fencing.
  • 05 Workload Isolation — public cloud broadly permitted; FISC guidelines drive isolation and key separation.
  • 06 Cross-Border Data Flow — APPI: consent, adequacy or safeguards; EU–Japan mutual adequacy in place.

02 — Resolution-planning and records-retention obligations often drive de facto onshore copies. 03 — BOJ-NET and Zengin (RTGS / ACH) must be operated by Japanese-licensed operators; DR is typically a second domestic region in a different seismic zone (FISC guidance).

04 — Subsidiaries required for certain securities / insurance business; ring-fencing is implicit through the FSA Comprehensive Supervisory Guidelines. 05 — privileged-access management, segregation of duties and production / non-production separation explicitly prescribed; zero-trust gaining traction via FSA cyber strategy.

06 — APPI amended 2022; FSA may attach extra supervisory conditions in outsourcing approvals.

Jurisdiction 4 of 10 · Domains 07–12

Japan (2/3)

  • 07 Cybersecurity Standards — FISC Guidelines at examination; FSA threat-led testing; <24 h reporting.
  • 08 API & Open Banking — Banking Act 2017 open-API: contractual EPSP model, no central mandate.
  • 09 Cloud & Infrastructure — FSA notification for material outsourcing; ISMAP-listed CSPs referenced.
  • 10 Licensing & Compliance Tech — APTCP AML; eKYC via selfie + ID or My Number IC chip; JAFIC as FIU.
  • 11 Encryption & Authentication — TLS 1.2+; CRYPTREC informs algorithms; MFA for high-risk; onshore HSM.
  • 12 Key Legislation — Banking Act, APPI (2022), FIEA, FISC Guidelines 10th edition (2024 revisions).

07 — FISC Security Guidelines (10th edition series) are the de facto examination standard; FSA Cybersecurity Policy / Practical Guidelines (updated 2024) set threat-led testing for G-SIBs / D-SIBs; reporting is "without delay", in practice under 24 hours for material events; ISO 27001 / NIST CSF mapping and PCI-DSS are common.

08 — Banks publish APIs under contracts with Electronic Payment Service Providers; JBA API standards; FAPI alignment growing — bilateral rather than a centralized CDR-style scheme.

09 — Exit / reversibility plans expected; concentration risk highlighted in supervisory communications. 11 — no mandated national algorithm family.

Jurisdiction 4 of 10 · Architectural Implications

Japan (3/3)

  • Anchor core banking, BOJ-NET and Zengin connectivity on-soil; DR in a second seismic region.
  • Map CSP controls to the FISC Security Guidelines — the lingua franca of FSA examination.
  • Use EU–Japan adequacy for group analytics on that axis; avoid mixing non-adequate regions.
  • Integrate My Number / JPKI for eKYC; design onboarding with pluggable identity evidence.
  • Publish open-API endpoints at FAPI grade; formalise EPSP partner onboarding as change management.

Japan is light on statute but heavy on examination practice — FISC alignment is what the supervisor actually tests against, which is why it functions as the control taxonomy for cloud architecture here.

Jurisdiction 5 of 10 · Domains 01–06

China (Mainland) (1/3)

  • 01 Regulatory Bodies — NFRA (prudential), PBOC (payments), CSRC, CAC (cyber & data), SCA (cryptography).
  • 02 Data Localization — CSL: CIIOs store personal and important data onshore; PBOC keeps financial data onshore.
  • 03 System & Process Localization — CNAPS, NUCC, UnionPay, NetsUnion clearing onshore; onshore DR and ops.
  • 04 Legal Entity Segregation — local subsidiary / JV licensing; the system of record must be local.
  • 05 Workload Isolation — MLPS 2.0 grades 1–5; financial core is Level 3+ on dedicated environments.
  • 06 Cross-Border Data Flow — PIPL routes: CAC assessment, standard contract, or certification.

01 — NFRA formed 2023, absorbing CBIRC; MPS enforces MLPS. 02 — PIPL and DSL (both 2021) reinforce onshore storage and add cross-border assessment regimes; bank-card transactions stay on UnionPay rails onshore; the CIIP Regulation (2021) formalises CIIO designation.

03 — Admin / DevOps access from outside China is generally disallowed for CIIO workloads — onshore operations teams required; "two-location, three-centre" multi-active DR patterns are standard.

04 — Securities / fund management usually needs a JV or WFOE with specific licences; local CIO / CISO and IT governance board are supervisory norms. 05 — public cloud only for non-CIIO workloads from licensed domestic CSPs. 06 — March 2024 CAC regulations relaxed thresholds, but they still apply to financial data; "important data" catalogues are in flux.

Jurisdiction 5 of 10 · Domains 07–12

China (Mainland) (2/3)

  • 07 Cybersecurity Standards — MLPS 2.0 (GB/T 22239), CIIP Regulation, JR/T 0071; immediate CAC reporting.
  • 08 API & Open Banking — no unified scheme; PBOC JR/T 0185 sets API security; NetsUnion routes super-apps.
  • 09 Cloud & Infrastructure — foreign CSPs only via licensed partners (AWS × Sinnet, Azure × 21Vianet).
  • 10 Licensing & Compliance Tech — AML Law (amended 2024); MPS ID verification for eKYC; e-CNY expected.
  • 11 Encryption & Authentication — SM2 / SM3 / SM4 mandated; SCA-certified HSMs; keys onshore.
  • 12 Key Legislation — CSL 2017, DSL 2021, PIPL 2021, CIIP 2021, Crypto Law 2020, CAC flow rules 2024.

07 — Incident reporting to CAC and sector regulators under CSL is immediate / within hours for material events; regular "HW / 护网" attack-defence drills organised by MPS and industry.

08 — Alipay and WeChat Pay act as quasi-open-access rails under PBOC supervision. 09 — data sovereignty is absolute for CIIOs; NFRA / PBOC outsourcing rules require notification or approval for material outsourcing; JR/T 0201 drives cloud-native modernisation.

10 — PBOC CAMLMAC reporting; facial recognition common in eKYC. 11 — Commercial Cryptography Law (2020) with SM9 also in the family; payment messaging typically signed SM2/SM3; MFA for customer and privileged access. 12 — Counter-Espionage Law amended 2023 also relevant.

Jurisdiction 5 of 10 · Architectural Implications

China (Mainland) (3/3)

  • Fully onshore stack — licensed domestic CSP or JV, SM-family crypto, SCA-certified HSMs, onshore SRE; assume no foreign admin access.
  • Design MLPS Level-3+ controls from day one — graded testing, dedicated SecOps, MPS go-live approval.
  • Treat cross-border data as a compliance project: map "important data", budget CAC assessment lead times, minimise egress.
  • Integrate CNAPS, NUCC, NetsUnion and UnionPay via approved domestic connectivity; roadmap e-CNY for retail.
  • Local entity, local CIO / CISO, local board, local system of record — offshore platforms cannot be the book of record.

Egress minimisation in practice means tokenisation and aggregation before anything leaves the mainland estate. The go-live approval from MPS for rated systems is a schedule dependency most global programmes underestimate.

Jurisdiction 6 of 10 · Domains 01–06

Hong Kong SAR (1/3)

  • 01 Regulatory Bodies — HKMA (banking & payments), SFC (securities & virtual assets), IA, PCPD (privacy).
  • 02 Data Localization — none statutory; records "readily available" in HK; PDPO s.33 not in force.
  • 03 System & Process Localization — CHATS RTGS and FPS onshore; offshore processing under SA-2 non-objection.
  • 04 Legal Entity Segregation — local incorporation or branch; Manager-in-Charge names accountable individuals.
  • 05 Workload Isolation — cloud accepted (HKMA Circular, Aug 2022); logical isolation, customer-managed keys.
  • 06 Cross-Border Data Flow — PDPO: consent plus safeguards; GBA pilots create mainland arrangements.

02 — PCPD guidance encourages the recommended Model Clauses even though s.33 is dormant; the mainland regime may bite on HK entities handling Chinese customers' data.

03 — CHATS and FPS run under HKMA / HKICL with direct onshore connectivity for participants; DR is typically intra-territory plus a regional secondary with documented site-resilience.

05 — The 2022 circular also introduced the Shared Assessments Programme for CSP due-diligence reuse; zero-trust / segmentation aligns with the C-RAF framework. 06 — GBA pilots include wealth-management connect and mBridge e-CNY experiments.

Jurisdiction 6 of 10 · Domains 07–12

Hong Kong SAR (2/3)

  • 07 Cybersecurity Standards — CFI 2.0: C-RAF, iCAST attack simulation, CIR development; <24 h reporting.
  • 08 API & Open Banking — Open API Framework Phases I–IV (transactional rolling out); CDI for SME data.
  • 09 Cloud & Infrastructure — SA-2 non-objection for material outsourcing; rehearsed exit plans.
  • 10 Licensing & Compliance Tech — AMLO / JFIU; biometric remote onboarding; iAM Smart digital identity.
  • 11 Encryption & Authentication — TLS 1.2+ per TM-E-1; strong customer authentication; FIPS HSMs onshore.
  • 12 Key Legislation — Banking Ordinance, PDPO, SPM TM-E-1 / SA-2, CFI 2.0, Cloud Circular 2022.

07 — CFI 2.0's three pillars: C-RAF assessment, iCAST intelligence-led attack simulation, CIR professional development; SFC and IA publish their own cyber codes; significant disruption is reportable immediately, other material events "as soon as practicable" (<24 h in practice).

08 — CDI (Commercial Data Interchange) shares consented corporate data across banks to accelerate SME credit; API security is OAuth 2.0, mTLS, signed JWTs.

09 — Concentration-risk assessment formalised; AWS / Azure / Alibaba HK regions widely used. 10 — remote onboarding per HKMA 2019/2020 guidance; SFC VATP licensing (Jun 2023) covers virtual-asset onboarding. 11 — 2FA is a long-standing norm; onshore HSMs preferred for regulator access.

Jurisdiction 6 of 10 · Architectural Implications

Hong Kong SAR (3/3)

  • Onshore HK regions for customer data, CHATS / FPS connectivity and the primary system of record; regional DR with proven recovery.
  • Design to C-RAF and iCAST from the outset — threat modelling and SOC maturity belong in the target state.
  • Expose Open-API Phase III/IV endpoints at FAPI grade, with CDI integration for SME credit journeys.
  • Use iAM Smart for consented identity and remote onboarding; fail open to branch where assurance is insufficient.
  • GBA flows: hold mainland customer data to PRC-grade controls — never blend it into the HK-only estate.

PRC-grade controls for GBA data means SM-family cryptography and PIPL treatment. The failure mode is a single blended data estate where mainland-origin records silently inherit HK-only controls.

Jurisdiction 7 of 10 · Domains 01–06

Singapore (1/3)

  • 01 Regulatory Bodies — MAS (integrated regulator & central bank), PDPC (privacy), CSA (national cyber).
  • 02 Data Localization — no mandate; cross-border processing permitted with timely regulator access.
  • 03 System & Process Localization — MEPS+, FAST, PayNow onshore; offshore processing under controls.
  • 04 Legal Entity Segregation — licence classes per activity; group outsourcing with local CIO / CISO.
  • 05 Workload Isolation — multi-tenant cloud fully accepted; MAS TRM 2021 controls; Cloud Advisory 2021.
  • 06 Cross-Border Data Flow — PDPA: consent plus comparable protection; ASEAN MCCs; APEC CBPR.

02 — MAS Notice 658 and outsourcing notices require records retrievable in Singapore with the FI keeping legal custody; CSP representations and warranties expected.

03 — Domestic rails run under MAS / ABS governance; DR is principle-based and proportionate — offshore DR allowed if risk-managed with demonstrable recovery to an onshore or equivalently controlled site.

04 — Full banks, wholesale banks and DPT licensees under the Payment Services Act 2019; significant FIs need a locally accountable Board IT Committee. 05 — TRM articulates IAM, segregation, encryption, logging; the Cloud Advisory covers concentration risk, exit strategy and continuity; zero-trust and API-gateway security are current supervisory expectations.

Jurisdiction 7 of 10 · Domains 07–12

Singapore (2/3)

  • 07 Cybersecurity Standards — TRM 2021; Notice 655 cyber hygiene; Notice 644: 1-hour incident reporting.
  • 08 API & Open Banking — SGFinDex via Singpass; MAS/ABS API Playbook (OAuth 2.0, FAPI, mTLS).
  • 09 Cloud & Infrastructure — outsourcing notices: regulator access, audit, exit clauses; concentration watched.
  • 10 Licensing & Compliance Tech — Notice 626 AML; Singpass / Myinfo eKYC; Singpass Face Verification.
  • 11 Encryption & Authentication — TLS 1.2+ with PFS; MFA universal, SMS-OTP phased out; FIPS L3+ HSMs.
  • 12 Key Legislation — Banking Act, Payment Services Act 2019, PDPA, Cybersecurity Act 2018, MAS TRM + Notices.

07 — Notice 655 covers patch, MFA, privilege, malware, secure configuration and security updates; Notice 644 requires a root-cause report within 14 days of the 1-hour notification; AASE adversarial attack simulation applies to systemically important FIs.

08 — SGFinDex spans banks, CPF, HDB, IRAS and insurers; open banking is facilitated and standardised rather than compelled. 10 — STRO is the FIU.

11 — Key management with dual control and split knowledge. 12 — the Notices family includes 655 / 644 / 634 / 1121 plus the Cloud Advisory 2021; the Cybersecurity Act 2018 carries the CII designation regime.

Jurisdiction 7 of 10 · Architectural Implications

Singapore (3/3)

  • Architect freely across regions — but evidence timely regulator access to records in Singapore, contractually and technically.
  • Bake TRM 2021 and Notice 655 controls into CI/CD — hardening baselines, patch SLAs, MFA, privileged access.
  • Engineer detection-to-notification as a pipeline: the 1-hour Notice 644 SLA needs runbooks and automation.
  • Build on Singpass / Myinfo for onboarding and SGFinDex for data sharing; FAPI-grade security is the minimum bar.
  • Prove exit: portability, data extraction and alternative-provider topologies — tabletop-tested and documented.

Singapore trades localization mandates for evidence obligations — the architecture question is not "where may it run" but "can you demonstrate access, control and exit on demand".

Jurisdiction 8 of 10 · Domains 01–06

Malaysia (1/3)

  • 01 Regulatory Bodies — BNM (banking & payments), SC (securities), JPDP (privacy), NACSA (cyber).
  • 02 Data Localization — BNM concurrence to store customer data offshore; onshore primary expected.
  • 03 System & Process Localization — RENTAS and DuitNow onshore (PayNet); offshore needs concurrence.
  • 04 Legal Entity Segregation — locally-incorporated subsidiaries under FSA / IFSA 2013; local CIO / CISO.
  • 05 Workload Isolation — RMiT: segregation; dedicated environments for core banking and payment switch.
  • 06 Cross-Border Data Flow — PDPA s.129 (2024): comparable protection or consent; BNM concurrence.

02 — The Outsourcing Policy Document (2019) requires BNM concurrence for offshore storage in material outsourcing; PDPA 2010 originally ran a whitelist of approved jurisdictions and the 2024 amendments moved to an adequacy model — implementation guidance still pending (Flux).

03 — DR is typically onshore for critical systems, regional for non-critical. 04 — board risk / technology committees expected; group platforms permitted with BNM-approved outsourcing and sub-outsourcing controls.

05 — RMiT (2020) prescribes production / non-production segregation and strong IAM / privileged access; multi-tenant cloud accepted with concurrence. 06 — cross-border outsourcing risk assessment covers geopolitical, legal and concentration risk.

Jurisdiction 8 of 10 · Domains 07–12

Malaysia (2/3)

  • 07 Cybersecurity Standards — RMiT as the comprehensive standard; Cyber Security Act 2024 adds CII regime.
  • 08 API & Open Banking — BNM Open Data & API policy (2018); PayNet DuitNow APIs; voluntary, FAPI-bound.
  • 09 Cloud & Infrastructure — BNM concurrence for material cloud; MY hyperscale regions arriving 2024–2026.

Jurisdiction 8 of 10 · Architectural Implications

Malaysia (3/3)

  • Default customer data and critical systems onshore; treat cross-border processing as a BNM-approval exercise.
  • Build to RMiT as the baseline controls catalogue; map patterns to its appendices for audit defensibility.
  • New Malaysian hyperscale regions make onshore cloud the path of least friction — revisit legacy offshore choices.
  • BNM-compliant e-KYC with liveness and document verification; engineer for MyDigital ID federation as it matures.
  • Align incident escalation to RMiT; anticipate Cyber Security Act 2024 CII licensing for CSPs and providers.

Every cross-border decision comes with a documented risk assessment and exit plan attached — BNM concurrence is a gate, not a formality. The arriving hyperscale regions flip the default: onshore is becoming cheaper than justifying offshore.

Jurisdiction 9 of 10 · Architectural Implications

Australia (3/3)

  • Adopt CPS 230 impact tolerances as first-class architectural inputs — they drive capacity, resilience and dependency design.
  • Build to CPS 234 control objectives mapped to APRA-expected evidence — the source of truth for assurance scope.
  • Expose CDR endpoints at FAPI 1.0 Advanced; treat the consent dashboard as a product-grade feature.
  • Register material service providers; pre-plan SOCI 12-hour / 72-hour incident workflows end-to-end.
  • Use the Digital ID Act 2024 federation as the strategic identity source; keep DVS / VoI fallback for edge cases.

Impact tolerances turn BCM from a document into a design constraint: each critical operation carries a tolerance that caps acceptable downtime and data loss, which flows straight into capacity and dependency choices. DSB register integration is part of the CDR product surface.
